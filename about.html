FACTS ABOUT MY HOME TOWN (GONDAR)

1. Capital of Ethiopia from the 17th century until 1868.

2. Noted center of ecclesiastical learning of the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church and known for having 44 churches.

3. It's known for the walled Fasil Ghebbi fortress and palace compound, once the seat of Ethiopian emperors.

4. Gondar is north of Tana Lake on the Lesser Angereb River and southwest of the Simien Mountains.

5. It has a latitude and longitude of 12°36′N 37°28′E with an elevation of 2133 meters above sea level.

6. The city holds the remains of several royal castles, including those in the Fasil Ghebbi UNESCO World Heritage Site for which Gondar has been called the "Camelot of Africa".

7. Timkat (which means "baptism" in Amharic) is the most important festival in the Ethiopian and specially this festival is highly celebrated in GONDER city.
